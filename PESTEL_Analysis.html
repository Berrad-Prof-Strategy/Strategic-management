General Environment (Macro-Environment) is all the variables that define the context where companies operate in a socio-economic system.

The general environment is different from one country to another. For example, a company working in Morocco (Moroccan or foreign) must understand how the variables of this socio-economic system work and how they can affect its activities.

Important: The company's results and even its survival depend on understanding the environment and the company's ability to adapt to it.

üí° Key Points to Remember:

1. Each Factor Has Many Aspects

Each type of PESTEL factor includes various elements. Each element covers different aspects and needs consideration of different variables.

Example: Economic factors include interest rates, but interest rates themselves involve consumer loans, business loans, savings rates, etc.

2. Need for Reliable Data

PESTEL analysis must be based on data from reliable sources. You must:

  • Determine which aspects and variables are relevant for your analysis
  • Identify sources that can provide quality data
  • Verify information from multiple sources when possible

3. Choose What Matters

Not all factors are equally important for every company. You must focus on the factors that have the biggest impact on your specific business.

üìã How to Conduct a Good PESTEL Analysis:

  • Step 1: Identify which PESTEL factors affect your industry
  • Step 2: Collect data from reliable sources (government reports, statistics, research studies)
  • Step 3: Analyze how each factor can create opportunities or threats
  • Step 4: Prioritize the most important factors for your company
  • Step 5: Update your analysis regularly because the environment changes
