üèóÔ∏è TYPES OF RESOURCES

1. Tangible Resources (Physical)

Things you can touch and see:

  • Financial: Money, profits, investments, ability to borrow
  • Physical: Buildings, factories, machines, equipment, land
  • Technological: Patents, licenses, databases, software systems

2. Intangible Resources (Non-Physical)

Things you cannot touch but are very valuable:

  • Brand reputation: Company image, customer trust, brand awareness
  • Human resources: Skills, knowledge, experience of employees
  • Organizational culture: Company values, way of working together

‚öôÔ∏è WHAT ARE CAPABILITIES?

Capabilities are the skills and abilities that a company develops to use its resources effectively. They show what a company does well.

Examples of capabilities:

  • Innovation capability: Creating new products or improving existing ones
  • Marketing capability: Understanding customers and promoting products well
  • Operations capability: Making products efficiently with good quality
  • Customer service capability: Helping customers and solving their problems quickly

üåü CORE COMPETENCIES & VRIO FRAMEWORK

Core competencies are the most important capabilities that make a company special and better than competitors. To be a true competitive advantage, resources and capabilities must pass the VRIO test:

VRIO Question to Ask
V
Valuable		 Does it help the company make money or reduce costs?
Does it help respond to opportunities or threats?
R
Rare		 Do only a few competitors have this resource or capability?
Is it hard to find in the market?
I
Inimitable		 Is it expensive or difficult for competitors to copy?
Would it take a long time to develop?
O
Organized		 Is the company organized to use this resource well?
Are the systems and processes in place?
üí° Key Point: Only resources and capabilities that pass all four tests (V-R-I-O) create a sustainable competitive advantage.

üè¢ MOROCCAN COMPANY EXAMPLES

Maroc Telecom (IAM)

Resources:

  • Extensive telecommunications network across Morocco
  • Large customer base (over 20 million subscribers)
  • Financial strength and investment capacity
  • Licensed spectrum for mobile and internet services
  • Retail stores and customer service centers nationwide

Capabilities:

  • Deploy and maintain telecommunications infrastructure efficiently
  • Manage large-scale network operations with high reliability
  • Adapt services to different customer segments (individuals, businesses)
  • Expand into digital services (mobile money, cloud services)
  • Build partnerships across Africa for regional growth

Core Competency:

Network infrastructure management - operating reliable telecommunications services across diverse geographic areas (cities, rural areas, mountains). This expertise took decades to develop and includes technical knowledge, relationships with suppliers, and understanding of local needs.

Cosumar

Resources:

  • Sugar production facilities in multiple locations
  • Agricultural land and partnerships with sugar beet farmers
  • Distribution network covering all Morocco
  • Strong brand names (Cosumar, Enmer, Sucrafor)
  • Technical expertise in sugar refining and processing

Capabilities:

  • Coordinate agricultural supply chain from farmers to factory
  • Maintain consistent product quality across all production sites
  • Optimize production costs through economies of scale
  • Manage seasonal variations in sugar beet and cane supply
  • Develop new sugar products for different customer needs

Core Competency:

Integrated sugar value chain management - controlling the entire process from working with farmers to delivering products to retailers. This integration allows Cosumar to ensure quality, control costs, and maintain stable supply. It took many years to build these relationships and systems.
